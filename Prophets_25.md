## 1 — Prophet Ādam عليه السلام

### Approximate era
- First human, first prophet.  
- Pre‑history — no meaningful BCE date.

### Age at death
- Classical reports (Ibn Kathīr, others): about **960 years**.

### Ancient location
- **Jannah** initially.  
- Then descended to earth; narrations differ:
  - Ādam: some say **India/Sri Lanka** region.  
  - Ḥawwā’: some say **Jeddah** area.  
  - They later reunited near **ʿArafāt**.

### Modern location equivalent
- **Sri Lanka / India / Arabian Peninsula** (various reports, not definitive).

### Family relations
- Wife: **Ḥawwā’ (Eve)**.  
- Children: many sons and daughters.  
- Notable son: **Shīth (Seth)**, considered a prophet by many scholars (not named in Qur’an).

### Nation’s sin
- No “nation” yet; only Ādam and his family.  
- His personal test:
  - Eating from the **forbidden tree**.  
  - Being deceived by Shayṭān.

### Nation destroyed?
- No nation to destroy.

### Repentance
- Ādam repented and Allah accepted:
  - **“Then Adam received from his Lord words, and He turned to him in mercy.”** (2:37)

### Key events & miracles
- Created from clay, directly by Allah.  
- Angels commanded to prostrate to him.  
- Taught the names of all things.  
- Lived in Paradise, then sent to earth.  
- Received words of repentance.

### Essential notes
- Father of all humanity.  
- His story establishes:
  - Human dignity.  
  - Human fallibility.  
  - The pattern: sin → regret → repentance → forgiveness.

## 2 — Prophet Idrīs عليه السلام

### Approximate era
- Several generations after Ādam, before Nūḥ.  
- Pre‑Flood prophet.

### Age at death
- Classical reports: around **865 years**.

### Ancient location
- Lived in **Babylon** (Iraq) according to many early scholars.  
- Later migrated to **Egypt**.

### Modern location equivalent
- **Iraq** (Babylon region).  
- **Egypt** (likely Nile valley).

### Family relations
- Lineage back to Ādam through Shīth.  
- Father: **Yard (Jared)**.  
- Grandfather: **Mahalalel**.  
- Son: **Matushala (Methuselah)**.

### Nation’s sin
- Early forms of:
  - Idolatry.  
  - Moral corruption.  
  - Injustice and oppression.

### Nation destroyed?
- No — Idrīs’s people were not destroyed as a whole.  
- The global destruction came later with Nūḥ.

### Repentance
- Some followed him; many did not.  
- No mass repentance like the people of Yūnus.

### Key events & miracles
- First to write with a **pen** (according to many reports).  
- First to sew clothes and wear stitched garments.  
- Associated with early **astronomy** and **calculation**.  
- Qur’an:  
  - **“And We raised him to a high place.”** (19:57)

### Essential notes
- Seen as a civilizational prophet — writing, measurement, order.  
- Some identify him with **Enoch** of Biblical tradition.  
- His “raising” is understood as a special honour, not necessarily like ʿĪsā’s.

## 3 — Prophet Nūḥ (Noah) عليه السلام

### Approximate era
- About **10 generations after Ādam** (Ibn Kathīr).  
- Often placed around **3,000–4,000 BCE** in historical reconstructions, but Islamic texts don’t give a date.

### Age at death
- Qur’an: he preached **950 years** to his people (29:14).  
- Many scholars estimate his total lifespan as **~1,000+ years**.

### Ancient location
- Region of **Mesopotamia**.  
- His people lived near the **Tigris/Euphrates**.

### Modern location equivalent
- **Iraq** — likely in the area of modern **Kufa / Basra / Mosul** (exact site unknown).

### Family relations
- Descendant of Idrīs and Ādam.  
- Sons: **Sām (Shem)**, **Hām (Ham)**, **Yāfith (Japheth)**.  
- All later prophets descend from **Sām**.

### Nation’s sin
- Deep, entrenched **idolatry**:
  - Worship of **Wadd, Suwāʿ, Yaghūth, Yaʿūq, Nasr** (71:23).  
- Mocking the prophet.  
- Persistent rejection over centuries.  
- Moral corruption and arrogance.

### Nation destroyed?
- **Yes — completely destroyed by the Flood**, except the believers on the Ark.

### Repentance
- Only a **small minority** believed.  
- No mass repentance; they persisted in disbelief until the punishment came.

### Key events & miracles
- Built the **Ark** under Allah’s command.  
- The **Great Flood** that drowned the disbelievers.  
- Animals taken in pairs.  
- Ark came to rest on **Mount Jūdī** (11:44) — in modern **Şırnak, Turkey**.  
- Humanity after him descends from the believers who were with him.

### Essential notes
- One of the **Ulū al‑ʿAzm** (five greatest prophets).  
- Symbol of **patience in daʿwah** — calling his people day and night for centuries.  
- His story is a warning about the consequences of **collective, stubborn disbelief**.

## 4 — Prophet Hūd عليه السلام

### Approximate era
- After Nūḥ, before Ibrāhīm.  
- Early post‑Flood Arabian prophet.

### Age at death
- Some reports: around **464 years**, but not strongly authenticated.

### Ancient location
- Sent to the people of **ʿĀd**.  
- They lived in **al‑Aḥqāf** — the sand dune region.

### Modern location equivalent
- **Southern Arabian Peninsula**:  
  - Likely **Yemen / Oman** region.  
  - Specifically linked to the **Rubʿ al‑Khālī (Empty Quarter)**.

### Family relations
- Descendant of Nūḥ through **Sām**.  
- Some reports say his full name was **Hūd ibn Shalikh ibn Arfakhshadh ibn Sām ibn Nūḥ**.

### Nation’s sin
- **Arrogance** due to physical strength and power.  
- **Idolatry**.  
- **Tyranny** and oppression.  
- Building monumental structures out of pride (26:128–129).  
- Rejection and mockery of Hūd.

### Nation destroyed?
- **Yes — destroyed by a furious, screaming wind**:  
  - Lasted **seven nights and eight days** (69:6–7).  
  - Left them like hollow, uprooted palm trunks.

### Repentance
- Only a **small group** believed and were saved with Hūd.  
- Majority persisted in disbelief.

### Key events & miracles
- Hūd warned them repeatedly, reminding them of Nūḥ’s people.  
- They accused him of foolishness and lying.  
- Allah saved Hūd and the believers from the wind.

### Essential notes
- ʿĀd were one of the **mightiest ancient nations**.  
- Their destruction is a recurring Qur’anic example of what happens when power + arrogance + disbelief combine.

## 5 — Prophet Ṣāliḥ عليه السلام

### Approximate era
- After Hūd, before Ibrāhīm.  
- Another early Arabian prophet.

### Age at death
- Some narrations: around **58 years**, but not firmly established.

### Ancient location
- Sent to the people of **Thamūd**.  
- They lived in **al‑Ḥijr**.

### Modern location equivalent
- **Madā’in Ṣāliḥ**, near **al‑ʿUlā**, in **north‑west Saudi Arabia**.

### Family relations
- Descendant of Nūḥ through Sām.  
- Some genealogies: **Ṣāliḥ ibn ʿUbayd ibn Āsif ibn Masikh ibn ʿUbayd ibn Ḥādhir ibn Thamūd**.

### Nation’s sin
- **Idolatry**.  
- **Arrogance** and pride in their engineering and carved homes.  
- **Cheating in trade** and injustice (similar to Shuʿayb’s people).  
- **Defiance**: demanding a miracle on their terms.  
- **Killing the she‑camel** that Allah sent as a sign.

### Nation destroyed?
- **Yes — destroyed by a mighty blast (ṣayḥah)** and earthquake.  
- Their homes remained, but they were wiped out.

### Repentance
- Only a **small group** believed and were saved with Ṣāliḥ.  
- The majority rejected him and killed the she‑camel.

### Key events & miracles
- The **she‑camel** miraculously emerged from a rock.  
- They were commanded to let her graze and drink on specified days.  
- They hamstrung and killed her despite warnings.  
- Allah destroyed them shortly after.

### Essential notes
- Their rock‑carved dwellings still exist and are visited today.  
- The Prophet ﷺ forbade entering their ruins except in a state of **fear and reflection**, not tourism‑style admiration.  
- Thamūd are a key Qur’anic example of people who **demanded a sign, saw it, then still disbelieved**.

# **6 — Prophet Ibrāhīm (Abraham) عليه السلام**

### **Approximate era**
- Historically placed around **2000–2100 BCE** (Middle Bronze Age).  
- Islamic texts do not give dates; this is based on ancient Near Eastern chronology.

### **Age at death**
- Classical Islamic historians: **~175 years**.

### **Ancient location**
- Born in **Ur** (Mesopotamia).  
- Preached in **Babylon**.  
- Migrated to **Harran**.  
- Settled in **Canaan**.  
- Travelled to **Egypt**.  
- Left Hājar and Ismāʿīl in **Bakkah (Makkah)**.

### **Modern location equivalent**
- **Ur** → Southern Iraq (near Nasiriyah).  
- **Babylon** → Central Iraq (Hillah).  
- **Harran** → Şanlıurfa, Turkey.  
- **Canaan** → Palestine/Israel.  
- **Egypt** → Nile Delta.  
- **Bakkah** → Makkah, Saudi Arabia.

### **Family relations**
- Father: **Āzar** (Qur’anic name).  
- Wives: **Sārah**, **Hājar**.  
- Sons: **Ismāʿīl**, **Isḥāq**.  
- Grandson: **Yaʿqūb**.  
- All Israelite prophets descend from **Isḥāq**.  
- Prophet Muḥammad ﷺ descends from **Ismāʿīl**.

### **Nation’s sin**
Ibrāhīm confronted multiple corrupt societies:
- **Idolatry** (sun, moon, stars, statues).  
- **Tyranny** (Nimrod).  
- **Priestly corruption** in Babylon.  
- **Moral decay** in Canaan.

### **Nation destroyed?**
- Babylon was not destroyed at that time.  
- Nimrod was destroyed individually (according to many reports).  
- The people of Lūṭ (his nephew) were destroyed later.

### **Repentance**
- Only a small number believed: Sārah, Lūṭ, and a few followers.

### **Key events & miracles**
- Breaking the idols.  
- Thrown into a massive fire — Allah made it cool.  
- Migration for Allah’s sake.  
- Angels visiting him.  
- Building the Kaʿbah with Ismāʿīl.  
- The sacrifice test.  
- Hosting angels with perfect hospitality.

### **Essential notes**
- Called **Khalīlullāh** (the intimate friend of Allah).  
- His life forms the foundation of **ḥajj**.  
- Central figure in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.

# **11 — Prophet Shuʿayb عليه السلام**

### **Approximate era**
- Lived after Ibrāhīm and before Mūsā.  
- Historically placed around **1600–1500 BCE**, though Islamic texts do not give dates.

### **Age at death**
- Classical reports vary; many place his lifespan around **~105–120 years**, but this is not firmly authenticated.

### **Ancient location**
- Sent to the people of **Madyan** and later addressed the people of **Aykah**.  
- Madyan was a major trading city on the ancient incense route.

### **Modern location equivalent**
- **North‑west Saudi Arabia**, near the modern city of **Tabuk**.  
- Some sites extend into **southern Jordan**.

### **Family relations**
- Many scholars say he was a **descendant of Ibrāhīm** through his wife Keturah (not through Sārah or Hājar).  
- Known as **“the preacher of the prophets”** due to his eloquence.

### **Nation’s sin**
The people of Madyan were guilty of:
- **Cheating in weights and measures** (a major theme in the Qur’an).  
- **Economic corruption**.  
- **Banditry and highway robbery**.  
- **Idolatry**.  
- **Threatening Shuʿayb** with expulsion or stoning.  
- **Manipulating markets** and exploiting travellers.

### **Nation destroyed?**
- **Yes — destroyed by a severe earthquake and a mighty blast (ṣayḥah)**.  
- The people of Aykah were destroyed by **intense heat and a scorching punishment**.

### **Repentance**
- Only a small group believed.  
- The majority rejected him and were destroyed.

### **Key events & miracles**
- His **eloquence** is highlighted in the Qur’an.
