The full duʿā’ you’re asking about is almost certainly the one the Prophet ﷺ taught to **Juwayriyyah bint al‑Ḥārith (رضي الله عنها)** — often spelled *Juwairiyah*, *Juwairiya*, or *Juria* in English.

This is a **well‑known, authentic** narration found in *Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim*. It is one of the most powerful morning adhkār.

---

## 🌿 The Duʿā’ Taught to Our Mother Juwayriyyah (رضي الله عنها)

### **Arabic**
**سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ، عَدَدَ خَلْقِهِ، وَرِضَا نَفْسِهِ، وَزِنَةَ عَرْشِهِ، وَمِدَادَ كَلِمَاتِهِ**

### **Transliteration**
*Subḥānallāhi wa bi‑ḥamdihī, ʿadada khalqihī, wa riḍā nafsihī, wa zinata ʿarshihī, wa midāda kalimātihī.*

### **Meaning**
“Glory is to Allah and praise is to Him,  
by the number of His creation,  
and by His pleasure,  
and by the weight of His Throne,  
and by the ink of His words.”

---

## 🌙 Context of the Hadith  
Juwayriyyah (رضي الله عنها) was sitting in the morning remembering Allah. The Prophet ﷺ told her that he had said **four phrases** three times which outweigh everything she had been saying:

> “I said **three times** words that would outweigh all that you have said since morning…”  
Then he taught her this duʿā’.

This is recorded in **Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim**.

---

## 📌 Why This Duʿā’ Is Special  
- It multiplies dhikr to an unimaginable scale.  
- It is from the *morning adhkār*, but can be said anytime.  
- It combines tasbīḥ (glorification) and ḥamd (praise).  
- It was personally taught by the Prophet ﷺ to one of the Mothers of the Believers.

## 🌙 Hadith of ʿĀ’ishah (RA) About the Duʿā’ for Laylat al‑Qadr

### **Full Hadith (English)**
ʿĀ’ishah (may Allah be pleased with her) reported:

> *“I said: O Messenger of Allah, if I know which night is Laylat al‑Qadr, what should I say in it?”*  
> *He said: “Say: O Allah, You are Pardoning and You love to pardon, so pardon me.”*

## 3) Say the Nightly Duʿā’ of Submission

### **Arabic**
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْلَمْتُ نَفْسِي إِلَيْكَ، وَوَجَّهْتُ وَجْهِي إِلَيْكَ، وَفَوَّضْتُ أَمْرِي إِلَيْكَ، وَأَلْجَأْتُ ظَهْرِي إِلَيْكَ، رَغْبَةً وَرَهْبَةً إِلَيْكَ، لَا مَلْجَأَ وَلَا مَنْجَا مِنْكَ إِلَّا إِلَيْكَ، آمَنْتُ بِكِتَابِكَ الَّذِي أَنْزَلْتَ، وَبِنَبِيِّكَ الَّذِي أَرْسَلْتَ

### **Transliteration**
*Allāhumma innī aslamtu nafsī ilayk, wa wajjahtu wajhī ilayk, wa fawwaḍtu amrī ilayk, wa alja’tu ẓahrī ilayk, raghbatan wa rahbatan ilayk, lā malja’a wa lā manjā minka illā ilayk, āmantu bikitābika alladhī anzalta wa binabiyyika alladhī arsalta.*

### **Translation**
“O Allah, I submit myself to You, I turn my face to You, I entrust my affairs to You, and I rely completely upon You, in hope and fear of You. There is no refuge and no escape from You except to You. I believe in Your Book which You revealed and Your Prophet whom You sent.”
